## Pattern Analysis from Goodreads Data (2012-2025)

### What Consistently Works (5-Star Patterns)

**The Lonesome Dove Standard**
- Only series with perfect 5-star sweep (all 4 books)
- Validates generational scope + character bonds over decades + bittersweet resonance = gold
- Your benchmark for what "disappointed it ended" means

**Long-Running Detective Series**
Multiple 5-star books across years:
- Harry Bosch series (Connelly) - multiple 5 stars from 2019-2020 era
- Cormoran Strike (Galbraith) - book #7 still earning 5 stars in 2024

**Pattern:** Long-form character development across many books works when quality maintains. Not episodic adventures but deepening relationships and stakes.

**Correction on Dugoni:** Goodreads tracked as 5 stars, but actual assessment is 3/5 - readable but not anticipated reads.

**Space Opera with Substance**
- The Expanse book #3 (Abaddon's Gate) = 5 stars in 2025
- The Last Emperox (Scalzi) = 5 stars in 2021
- Wool (Howey) = 5 stars in 2025

**Pattern:** Space opera + political intrigue + character depth = reliable hits

**Epic Fantasy World-Building**
- Words of Radiance (Sanderson) - 5 stars
- Multiple Sanderson books rated 4 stars across years

**Pattern:** 800+ page epic fantasy with intricate systems works when executed well

### What Consistently Fails (1-2 Star Patterns)

**Hard Dealbreakers (These Kill Books)**
1. **Episodic/monster-of-week structure**
   - The Last Wish (Witcher) = 2 stars
   - Storm Front (Dresden Files) = 2 stars
   - American Gods (multiple storylines) = 1 star

2. **Alienating/too-experimental atmospheres**
   - American Gods = 1 star
   - The Slow Regard of Silent Things = 2 stars

3. **Too cold/distant narration**
   - Tinker, Tailor, Soldier, Spy = 2 stars
   - Despite spy thriller genre fit, Le Carré's style doesn't work

4. **Unlikable protagonists**
   - Prince of Thorns = 2 stars ("too much of a dick")
   - Last Bus to Woodstock = 1 star (series didn't resonate)

**"Should Have Worked" Failures (Genre Fit But Execution Wrong)**
- The Son (Meyer) = 2 stars - weirdly written despite being generational saga with American history
- Best Served Cold (Abercrombie) = 2 stars - grimdark execution didn't connect
- The Housemaid = 2 stars - thriller premise but poor execution
- Dark Places (Flynn) = 2 stars - psychological thriller with issues
- Winter World = 2 stars - sci-fi concept that didn't work

**Key Insight:** Genre fit isn't enough. Execution matters more than premise.

### Series Commitment Insights

**Worth Multi-Book Investment:**
- Lonesome Dove tetralogy - all 4 books earned 5 stars
- Harry Bosch (through ~book 18) - consistent 5 stars
- Cormoran Strike (through book 7) - maintaining quality
- The Expanse (through book 3) - strong consistency

**Series Fatigue Visible:**
- Later Connelly books (2020s) show quality decline
- Pattern: "loosely connected stories, feels like combined short stories"

**One-and-Done Failures:**
- Last Bus to Woodstock (Inspector Morse) = 1 star → didn't try series
- Storm Front (Dresden Files) = 2 stars → series abandoned
- Prince of Thorns = 2 stars → didn't continue

**Recommendation Strategy:**
- Safe bet: Continue series with proven 5-star track record
- Risky: Start new series from authors with inconsistent quality
- High confidence: Standalone books from reliable 5-star authors

**Consistent Quality (Multiple 5-Stars):**
- Michael Connelly (early-to-mid career)
- Brandon Sanderson (multiple 4-5 star books)
- J.K. Rowling/Galbraith (Strike series maintaining)

**Declined Over Time:**
- Michael Connelly (post-2020 work shows fatigue)

**Variable Quality (Some Books Excellent, Others Missable - Need Specific Title Guidance):**

- Gillian Flynn (Dark Places = 2 stars)

### Genre-Specific Success Rates

**Highest Success: Crime/Detective Series**
- Multiple 5-star books (Connelly, Galbraith)
- Long-form character development works consistently
- Recommend with high confidence when quality track record exists
- Note: Not all detective series reach this level (Dugoni = 3/5, readable but not anticipated)

**Solid Success: Space Opera**
- The Expanse, Scalzi, Howey all delivering 5 stars
- Political intrigue + character depth = reliable

**Moderate Success: Epic Fantasy**
- Sanderson delivers but not always 5 stars
- Need substantial world-building + character investment

**Variable Success: Thrillers**
- Grisham: Wide range - early production and Sycamore Row series = 4.5/5 excellent, but Rogue Lawyer = 1 star, The Confession/The Associate = 2 stars
- Flynn = 2 stars (Dark Places)
- Need careful vetting and specific title guidance before recommending

**Avoid: Urban Fantasy**
- Dresden Files = 2 stars
- Pattern: Forced humor + episodic structure = fail
