## Matias's Reading Profile

### Core Preferences

- **Emotional impact + immersive world/plot** are top priorities
- **LOVES generational sagas** - stories spanning across decades or generations
- Loves long books and series - Stormlight-length commitment is welcome
- Language: Finnish native, fluent English - mostly reads English (easier to find ebooks)
- Format: Ebooks on Onyx Boox for fiction; audiobooks (Storytel) for non-fiction only

### The "Ending Feeling" Test

This is how Matias measures book quality:

- **Great book:** Disappointed it ends - you want more (Galbraith, Lonesome Dove)
- **Meh book:** No particular feelings - passed the time but not inspiring (LJ Ross)
- **Bad book:** Relieved it's over - or stopped midway (Peter James Dead Simple)

### DNF Philosophy

"Life is too short for bad books." Will give a book ~100 pages, but if it feels like a chore, stops. Used to push through; now prioritizes enjoyment over completion. Doesn't matter if he finishes - would rather put a bad book down than plough through.

### What Makes a Perfect Book for Matias

- **Generational scope** - stories that span decades or generations (Lonesome Dove, Godfather, Shōgun pattern)
- **Deep character bonds** and relationships that develop over time
- **Bittersweet emotional resonance** - not afraid of mortality and loss; ending should hurt in a good way
- **Sense of place** - landscape and setting as character (Western frontier, Japan, crime families)
- **Length that allows full immersion** - 800+ pages is a feature, not a bug
- **Accessible atmosphere** even in speculative fiction - immersion, not alienation
- **Moral complexity** - no pure good or pure evil, just shades of both; villain POV welcome
- **Complex, lived-in worlds** (like GRRM's Westeros, Sanderson's Cosmere)
- **Slow burn pacing** - patient worldbuilding and character development; 200+ pages setup is fine
- **Connected narrative arcs** - NOT episodic/monster-of-week structure (deal-breaker)
- **Resonant protagonists** - needs emotional connection to main character(s)
- **Historical/cultural immersion** - when done right (Shōgun, Lonesome Dove), this elevates everything
- **Family dynamics and loyalty themes** - Godfather, Lonesome Dove pattern; brotherhood, chosen family

### Content Preferences

- **Violence level:** GRRM-level or higher is fine - grimdark, explicit violence, dark themes welcome
- **Romance:** Tolerates it when well-executed (like Galbraith), doesn't seek it out
- **Tone:** Serious preferred; forced humor or quippy protagonists don't land
- **Villain POV:** YES - loves getting inside antagonist heads; reinforces "no one is truly evil, all have their viewpoint"
- **Betrayal:** Prefers genuine surprises but earned buildup works too; Red Wedding is a benchmark moment
- **Political stakes:** Must feel real - schemes with genuine teeth, not court drama for its own sake

### What DOESN'T Work

- **Episodic/monster-of-week structure** (why Witcher/Dresden failed)
- **Protagonists that don't resonate emotionally** (can't connect = DNF)
- **Humor that feels forced** or doesn't land
- **Alienating/abnormal atmospheres** (Liu, Reynolds, Jemisin's ground-traveling creatures)
- **Irredeemable edgelord protagonists** (Prince of Thorns - "too much of a dick")
- **Infantile/unbelievable writing** - plots that feel written by 15-year-old (Dicker post-Harry Quebert); requires mature, grounded storytelling
- **Later-series decline** - books that feel like combined short stories, loosely connected, author running out of ideas (late Connelly, new Corey series)
- **Inconsistent author quality** - needs reliable quality or clear guidance on which specific titles work (Colson Whitehead problem)
- One book should equal one cohesive story arc
